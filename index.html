Inequality

The following chart depicts the historical trend of global income inequality from 1820 to 2013.

For much of history, the majority of the population lived in extreme poverty. This pattern began to change with economic growth. Poverty headcount ratio shows that global poverty declined from 94% in 1820 to almost 10% in 2013. The majority of poor people live in Sub-Saharan Africa, and South Asia.
The long run of historical data on inequality shows us that there have been periods in the past when inequality fell and when poverty was reduced (1840-1860). On the other hand, despite recent progress, since the last quarter of a century, income inequality is at its highest level.
